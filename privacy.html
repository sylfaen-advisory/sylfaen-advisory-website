01 — Who we are

The data controller

Sylfaen Advisory Limited is a construction assurance consultancy registered in Wales, United Kingdom. We provide information management consultancy services, including CDE environment design, ISO 19650 compliance advisory, and data assurance engagements.

For the purposes of UK data protection law, Sylfaen Advisory Limited is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy. Our primary contact for data protection matters is Mark Biscoe, reachable at mark@sylfaenadvisory.co.uk.

02 — What we collect

Personal data we may hold

As a B2B consultancy, we collect and hold personal data relating to business contacts, prospective clients, and individuals at client organisations. This typically includes:

  • Names and job titles
  • Business email addresses
  • Business telephone numbers
  • Company or organisation names
  • Project or engagement context provided in enquiry forms or correspondence
  • Records of communications and meeting notes

We do not collect or store sensitive personal data (as defined under UK GDPR Article 9), financial account details, or personal data relating to individuals who are not acting in a professional or business capacity.

03 — Legal basis

Why we are permitted to process your data

We process personal data on the basis of legitimate interests (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f)), specifically the legitimate interests of running a professional B2B consultancy practice. These interests include:

  • Responding to business enquiries and evaluating engagement opportunities
  • Delivering contracted consultancy services to client organisations
  • Maintaining records of professional relationships for continuity of service
  • Communicating relevant practice updates to individuals who have engaged with us
We have assessed that our legitimate interests are not overridden by your rights and interests, given the strictly B2B context of our work. You have the right to object to processing at any time — see Section 07.
04 — How we use your data

Purposes of processing

We use personal data for the following purposes:

  • Responding to and managing engagement enquiries
  • Planning, delivering, and following up on consultancy engagements
  • Sending engagement letters, proposals, and project correspondence
  • Maintaining records required for professional indemnity insurance purposes
  • Occasional direct contact about practice news relevant to your professional role — you may opt out at any time

We will not use your personal data for automated decision-making or profiling, and we will not send unsolicited marketing communications.

05 — Retention

How long we keep your data

We retain personal data for three years from the date of last meaningful contact — defined as a response to a communication, an active engagement, or an explicit request to remain in contact.

Where we have a contractual or professional obligation to retain records for a longer period (for example, records relevant to a professional indemnity insurance claim), we will retain only the minimum data necessary for that purpose.

At the end of the retention period, personal data is securely deleted from our systems and from any processors acting on our behalf.

You may request erasure at any time — see Section 07. We will act on erasure requests within 30 days unless a legal or professional obligation prevents us from doing so, in which case we will explain the specific reason.

06 — Sharing your data

Third-party processors and disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes under any circumstances.

We use the following third-party processors who may handle personal data on our behalf under appropriate data processing agreements:

  • HubSpot, Inc. — CRM platform used to manage contact records and engagement correspondence. HubSpot processes data under a Data Processing Agreement and maintains EU/UK Standard Contractual Clauses for international transfers.
  • Formspree, Inc. — form submission processor used to receive enquiries submitted via this website. Data is forwarded to our email and deleted from Formspree servers after delivery.

We may disclose personal data to third parties where required by law, court order, or regulation, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Sylfaen Advisory Limited, our clients, or others.

07 — Your rights

Rights under UK GDPR

Under UK data protection law, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — ask us to delete your data where we have no overriding legitimate purpose or legal obligation to retain it
  • Right to restriction — ask us to restrict processing in certain circumstances, for example while a correction is being verified
  • Right to portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format where technically feasible
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests at any time; we will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at mark@sylfaenadvisory.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days. There is no charge for reasonable requests.

08 — Complaints

Your right to complain to the ICO

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection.

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Helpline: 0303 123 1113
  • Address: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

We would always prefer the opportunity to address concerns directly before a formal complaint is made — please contact us first if you are willing to do so.

09 — Changes

Updates to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date in the sidebar reflects the date of the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated to individuals whose data we hold where reasonably practicable.

The current version of this policy is always available at sylfaenadvisory.co.uk/privacy.html.
